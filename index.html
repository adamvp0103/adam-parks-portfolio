About Me

In 2020, after graduating from high school, I was doing some exploring online when I came across a Python coding tutorial for beginners on YouTube. Having never coded before, I was surprised how much you could achieve through programming and became obsessed with learning how to do it myself. From there, I entered Towson University's computer science program where I learned fundamental CS concepts, improved my algorithmic thinking, and familiarized myself with how hardware and software work.

I received by Bachelor's degree in computer science three years after joining the program. After graduating, I took some time to explore the different fields in the tech world. This is where I uncovered my passion for front-end web development. I had always been captivated by the internet and how it works, so learning how to contribute to it myself was very exciting. I've always enjoyed creating things, so being able to see a visual representation of my work was quite satisfying, and that aspect is what drew me to the front-end.

After familiarizing myself with the basics of front-end development, I decided to pursue a more structured education by completing the Meta Front-End Developer certification. These courses solidified many of the concepts I had been learning on my own and increased my confidence for working on my own projects.

Today, I am actively seeking a junior developer position as I continue to broaden my skill set at home. I plan on exploring some open-source projects on GitHub and continuing to build my portfolio, so be sure to check back soon!
